Symptoms of Schizophrenia


Delusions

  • false beliefs that are not based in reality
  • thinking that you're being harmed
  • something big is about to happen
  • happen in most people with very serious mental disorder

Hallucination

  • seeing or hearing things that don't exist.
  • Applies to all 5 senses
  • For a person with schizophrenia, they have the full force and impact of a normal experience
  • Hearing voices is the most common hallucination

Disorganized thinking

  • Disorganized thinking is inferred from disorganized speech
  • answers to questions may be partially or completely unrelated.
  • Rarely, speech may include putting together meaningless words that can't be understood, sometimes known as word salad.

Extremely disorganized or abnormal motor behavior

  • childlike silliness to unpredictable agitation
  • resistance to instructions
  • a complete lack of response
  • seless and excessive movement

Negative symptoms

  • reduced or lack of ability to function normally
  • neglecting personal hygiene or appearing to lack emotion
  • may lose interest in everyday activities
  • socially withdraw or lack the ability to experience pleasure
